What this is

The work that surrounds the medicine.

A psychedelic experience can crack something open — but what arrives in the experience is only the beginning. The integration work is the long, careful weaving: helping your nervous system land, helping the insights find a place in your actual life, and helping what was seen actually become how you live.

I work with you in the days, weeks, and months around an experience you're undertaking elsewhere. We prepare your body and intention before, hold the threshold during, and walk with you through the integration afterward.

I do not provide, recommend, or refer to psychedelic substances. I can offer referrals to reputable, legal facilitators if helpful.

How this unfolds

The arc of integration

Preparation

Two to six sessions in the weeks before. We clarify intention, ready your nervous system, name what might come up, and build the steadiness that lets you meet the experience with less reactivity and more presence.

The threshold

A session shortly before and shortly after — holding the edges of the experience itself. We don't go in cold, and we don't come out into nothing.

Integration

The longer arc — typically three to six months of regular sessions. Working with what arose, tending to what got stirred up, and helping the insights become embodied rather than just remembered.

The slow weave

Real integration is not a single conversation. It's the patient process of letting your relationships, choices, and rhythms slowly reorganize around what you've come to know.
