What this is

The truth your body has been holding.

Some things can't be reasoned through. They live below language — in the breath, the held muscle, the closed-off place that won't quite open when the mind asks it to. Somatic healing is the slow, careful work of meeting the body where it actually is, and offering the kind of presence that lets it begin to unwind.

I bring sixteen years of professional massage and deep-tissue practice into this work, woven together with somatic and nervous-system-informed approaches. These sessions are not about fixing the body. They're about listening to it — and helping the parts of you that have been bracing finally exhale.

What we work with

The territory of the body

Nervous-system regulation

Working with the body's stress responses — fight, flight, freeze, fawn — and gently teaching the system that it can come back down.

Body-stored trauma

The places where old experience is still held in tissue, breath, and posture. We don't force them open. We invite them.

Embodied safety

The kind of safety that isn't a thought — it's a physiology. The state your body remembers when something is actually okay.

Polyvagal awareness

Tracking what state you're in, what brings you back to ventral, and what keeps pulling you out — making the autonomic visible.

Breath & rhythm

The ways breath gets cut off when we brace, and the slow practice of letting it return — without forcing, without ritualizing.

Resourcing

Building an interior felt-sense of safety, steadiness, and capacity — the kind that travels with you outside the session.

How sessions unfold

The shape of the work

In-person bodywork (Austin, TX)

Hands-on somatic sessions integrating deep tissue, fascia release, and trauma-informed touch. Ninety minutes to two hours, on the table, with attention to whatever the body is asking to be met.

Somatic coaching (online)

For clients outside Austin, somatic work happens through video — guided body-tracking, breath, micro-movements, and resourcing practices, all in service of the same physiology.

Paced by your body

Nothing is forced. We work at the speed your nervous system can actually integrate. Sometimes that means going slow. Sometimes that means going slower than that.

Often woven with coaching

For many clients, somatic work pairs with the talking work — alternating sessions, or layered within the same hour. The body and the story belong to the same person.
